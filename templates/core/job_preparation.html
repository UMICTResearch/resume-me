Top Interview Tips:

At the time of the interview, you’ll want to remember these eight things according to U.S. News: Money.

  1. Dress appropriately - Know the environment in which you will be interviewing. If you have questions, refer to human resources at your target company about what’s appropriate. A general rule of thumb is a suit and tie for a man and the equivalent for a woman but many industries are more casual (e.g., shirt and tie for men and slacks and a nice top for women).
  2. Be on time—in fact, you may want to plan to arrive 5-10 minutes early.
  3. Know your own resume and make sure that everything on your resume is 100% true.
  4. Turn off your cell phone before you enter the building for an interview.
  5. Be professional at all times - Don’t let your guard down and use inappropriate vocabulary or a tasteless joke. Never say anything negative about your current place of employment.
  6. Be succinct - Before providing a long answer to a question, think about your answer to the question, plot out what’s important, say it, and then be quiet. Follow up only as needed.
  7. Be prepared at every stage of the game - Preparation is key for job interviews. Always prepare and investigate the company well enough to write a cover letter, which shows why you make a good fit for the particular position.
  8. Be engaged - Be aware of your body language; keep a good posture and avoid slouching in your seat. Always look at the person with whom you are speaking. Use tone and expression to show your passion and excitement for the work.

Interview Preparation:

Be sure to prepare for all upcoming interviews. The best way to study is to prepare strong answers to potential questions and practice your answers with a friend! Think through the questions and formulate answers ahead of time. Here are a few of the most common interview questions according to US News: Money:

  1. Tell me about yourself: Prepare a one-minute answer that summarizes where you are in your career and what you’re great at, with a focus on your most recent job. Most interviewers are not interested in your personal life so be careful about discussing information related to hobbies,your family, or details about where you grew up.
  2. What interests you about this job? Do not mention salary, benefits, or the short commute to work. Instead, focus on the work itself—not what the job can do for you.
  3. If you are leaving a job, you may be asked why. E.g., Why are you thinking about leaving your job? When answering this question, never discuss conflicts with your co-workers or manager or complain about your work. Never badmouth employers. The suggested answer is that you’re seeking new challenges but you must specify what these new challenges will be. It is also okay to mention things such as financial instability of the organization, or a planned move (be sure that these are true).

    4. Other common questions to consider:

  4. Why would you excel at this job?
  5. What do you know about the company so far?
  6. Tell me about a time when…(e.g., you took initiative, deal with difficult people, etc.)
